Troubleshooting and Debugging Techniques week 4

memory leak: memory that is no longer needed is not released.
garbage collector: in charge of freeing the memory that is no longer in use.

use profiler to figure out how the memory is being used.

latency: the time taken for a data packet to reach its destination after being sent. Affected by the physical distance and how many devices are in between. Transmitting many small pieces of data, aiming for less than 50ms. 
bandwidth: the amount of data that can be transmitted and received during a specific period of time. determined by each endpoint and hop between them. Transmitting larger data need bigger bandwidth.
sometimes people use the term “throughput” to inform you of the number of packets actually sent and received.

make a list of all tasks!

Eisenhower Matrix: split tasks into two categories: urgent and important.

estimating the time task will take: rough estimate with a similar task before, be realistic and note it down.

communicating expectations: give them an idea of what and when you are going to do. try to be clear and upfront. if it is hard to estimate, give them a timely update.

use a ticket tracking system.

technical debate: when we choose a quick and easy solution instead of a long-term one.

some tips to make our future lives easier:
1. keep it clear and simple to understand.
2. develop code in small digestible chunks, stop and test often.
4. keep the goal clear, list it down first.
4. KEEP CALM AND CARRY ON
5. ask for help, or maybe explain the problem to a rubber duck. not to tell them what you think at first, for them to come up with different paths.

more proactive practices:
1. have an infrastructure and good test that makes problem-solving easier. 
2. continuous integration and test environment.
3. deploy software in phases or canaries in case something went wrong.
4. having debugging logs, centralized logs collection, a good monitoring system, and ticketing systems.
5. document what you did.
6. monitoring resource usage and planning for the future.
7. migrate some parts or whole of systems to the cloud.
